Background and Goal

We recently updated the automated scripts that calculate Department response times for the Monthly Compstat Report. To ensure the data accurately reflects public calls for service, we are now filtering out 92 types of administrative and self-initiated activities (e.g., meal breaks, TAPS details, training, patrol checks). We are also ensuring that for multi-unit responses, the system only counts the arrival time of the first officer on scene.

Why we need your help: While the Emergency and Urgent response times look highly accurate, the "Routine" category still shows a very wide variation. Because dispatchers handle the reality of CAD data entry, we are asking for your operational expertise to review these numbers and tell us if there are CAD workflow habits or edge cases we missed.

Operational Anomaly The "Routine" Data Skew

While an average of 2:01 for Routine calls makes sense, a closer look at the data reveals a massive skew. The median (the exact middle number of all the calls) for Routine dispatch-to-scene is only 13 seconds.

Our Theory: We believe this is happening because self-initiated enforcement activities (like motor vehicle stops and field contacts) remain in the "Routine" dataset. In these instances, the officer has already stopped the vehicle or made contact before calling it in. Therefore, the CAD "Time Dispatched" and "Time Out" (On Scene) are logged almost simultaneously, resulting in a near-zero response time that drags the overall average down.

Dispatcher Feedback Questionnaire

Please review the questions below based on your day-to-day experience using CAD. Your insights will help us refine our data filters.

1. Motor Vehicle Stops & Field Contacts

Does our theory align with how you enter motor vehicle stops and field contacts? Are the "Dispatched" and "On Scene" timestamps usually hit back-to-back in these scenarios?

2. Other "Already On Scene" Scenarios

Besides MV stops and field contacts, are there other common "Routine" call types where the officer is already on location before you officially dispatch them in CAD?

3. Call Stacking and Holding

When stacking Routine calls for a busy unit, how does that affect timestamps? Is the "Time Received" logged immediately, but "Time Dispatched" held until the officer is free? Does this artificially inflate any of our processing times?

4. General Observations

Are there any other CAD entry workflows, status changes, or system quirks we should be aware of that might explain why the median Routine response time is so low?
